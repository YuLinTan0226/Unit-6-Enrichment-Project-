Factors Contributing to the Surplus

  • Semiconductor Dominance: Taiwan produces over 60% of global semiconductors and 90% of advanced chips
  • High-Tech Manufacturing: Advanced production capabilities in electronics and ICT equipment
  • Global AI Demand: Rising need for high-performance chips for AI applications
  • Specialized Expertise: Niche dominance in semiconductor manufacturing technology

Top 5 Imports

Goods/resources Taiwan imports that have major contributions to its economy:

  • Mineral Fuels (oil, gas, coal) - Energy dependency for manufacturing
  • Industrial Machinery - Capital equipment for advanced manufacturing
  • Vehicles & Automotive Parts - Consumer and industrial transport
  • Pharmaceuticals & Medical Supplies - Healthcare sector needs
  • Agricultural Products (soybeans, corn, beef) - Food security requirements

Import Significance

Taiwan is energy-dependent, importing over 98% of its energy needs. These imports support its massive manufacturing sector and domestic consumption.

Top 5 Exports

Goods/resources Taiwan exports that drive its economic growth:

  • Semiconductors & Integrated Circuits - 34.7% of total exports
  • Computers & Communication Equipment - ICT industry products
  • Precision Instruments & Machinery - High-tech manufacturing equipment
  • Plastics & Chemical Products - Industrial materials
  • Iron & Steel Products - Manufacturing base output

Export Significance

Semiconductors alone account for over one-third of Taiwan's total exports. This technological dominance makes Taiwan crucial to the global supply chain.

Historical Trade Trend (2014-2024)

Trend Analysis

Net exports have consistently increased over the past decade, with particular acceleration since 2020.

Post-COVID Acceleration

Since 2020, exports and imports have grown by approximately 10% annually, driven by increased demand for electronics during remote work and the AI boom.

AI & Technology Demand

The global artificial intelligence revolution has dramatically increased demand for Taiwan's high-performance semiconductors.

Geopolitical Factors

Taiwan has strategically focused on semiconductor dominance as an economic defense against regional pressures, investing heavily in R&D and manufacturing capacity.

Conclusion: Taiwan's trade surplus has grown steadily due to its specialized position in the global technology supply chain, particularly in semiconductors where it maintains a competitive advantage over other economies including China.
